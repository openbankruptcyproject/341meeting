Documents Needed for Your 341 Meeting

A printable checklist so you do not forget anything on meeting day

The 341 meeting of creditors requires specific documents. If you show up without them, the trustee will continue your meeting to a later date -- delaying your case and requiring a second appearance. This checklist covers everything you need.

Required Documents -- Every Case

These documents are required in virtually every bankruptcy district in the country. Bring originals, not copies, for identity verification.

Identity Documents (Bring Originals)

  • Government-issued photo ID -- driver's license, state ID card, U.S. passport, or military ID. It must be current (not expired) and show your photograph.
  • Proof of Social Security number -- Social Security card, W-2, or 1099 form that shows your full Social Security number. A partial number is not sufficient.

Financial Documents

  • Most recent federal tax return -- the full return, including all schedules and W-2s. Most trustees want the return for the most recently completed tax year. If you have not filed, you may need to file before the meeting.
  • Pay stubs for 60 days before filing -- all pay stubs from all employers for the 60-day period immediately before your bankruptcy petition date. Under 11 U.S.C. Section 521(a)(1)(B)(iv), these must be provided to the trustee at least 7 days before the meeting.
  • Bank statements -- 6 months -- statements for all bank accounts (checking, savings, money market) for the 6 months before filing. Some trustees require statements through the month of the meeting.

Commonly Required Additional Documents

Depending on your district, your trustee, and the specifics of your case, you may also need:

Property and Asset Documents

  • Vehicle titles or registration -- proof of ownership for all vehicles listed on your schedules
  • Real estate deed or mortgage statement -- if you own a home, the trustee may want to see the deed, most recent mortgage statement, or property tax assessment
  • Retirement account statements -- most recent statements for any 401(k), IRA, pension, or other retirement accounts
  • Life insurance policies -- if any policy has cash value, bring the policy and most recent statement

Income Verification

  • Social Security or disability award letter -- if you receive government benefits as a source of income
  • Unemployment compensation records -- if you are receiving or recently received unemployment benefits
  • Business income records -- if self-employed, bring profit and loss statements, 1099s, and business bank statements
  • Rental income documentation -- leases or receipts if you receive rental income

Legal Documents

  • Copy of your bankruptcy petition and schedules -- review these before the meeting so you can answer questions about them
  • Credit counseling certificate -- proof you completed the required pre-filing credit counseling under 11 U.S.C. Section 109(h)
  • Divorce decree or separation agreement -- if applicable, especially if domestic support obligations are involved
  • Prior bankruptcy records -- if you have filed bankruptcy before, bring the case number and filing date of any prior cases

What If You Are Missing a Document?

If you realize before the meeting that you are missing something, contact your attorney immediately. They may be able to:

  • Request a continuance before the meeting date
  • Obtain the document from the court or another source
  • Advise you on whether the trustee is likely to require it in your specific case

If you arrive at the meeting without a required document, the trustee will typically continue the meeting to a later date. This means you will need to attend a second meeting, which delays your case by 2 to 4 weeks in most districts.

Virtual Meeting Document Tips

If your 341 meeting is by Zoom or phone, document requirements work slightly differently:

  • Many trustees require you to email scans or photos of your ID and Social Security proof before the meeting
  • Some districts have a specific upload portal for pre-meeting documents
  • Have all documents accessible on your computer so you can share your screen if asked
  • Check your court's local procedures -- they vary by district

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need for my 341 meeting?

At minimum: a government-issued photo ID, proof of your Social Security number, your most recent tax return, 60 days of pay stubs, 6 months of bank statements, and a copy of your petition. Many trustees also require vehicle titles, mortgage statements, and retirement account statements depending on your case.

What happens if I forget to bring documents?

The trustee will likely continue your meeting to a later date. This delays your case. If you realize you are missing something, contact your attorney before the meeting.
