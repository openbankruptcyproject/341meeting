341 Meeting Trustee Questions List

Every question the trustee is likely to ask - organized by category so you can prepare

The bankruptcy trustee assigned to your case runs the 341 meeting of creditors. Their job is to verify your identity, confirm your paperwork is accurate, and look for any issues that need further investigation. The questions follow a predictable pattern in nearly every district.

Below is a comprehensive list of the questions trustees commonly ask, organized by topic. Not every trustee asks every question, but knowing all of them means nothing will catch you off guard.

Identity Verification Questions

Every 341 meeting begins with identity verification. The trustee must confirm you are who you say you are. These questions are required by the U.S. Trustee Program guidelines.

Standard Identity Questions

  1. Please state your full legal name for the record.
  2. What is your current address?
  3. Did you bring a valid government-issued photo ID?
  4. Did you bring proof of your Social Security number?
  5. Is the Social Security number on your petition correct?

You will need to show your ID and Social Security proof to the trustee. Acceptable ID includes a driver's license, state ID, or passport. Acceptable Social Security proof includes your Social Security card, a W-2, or a 1099 showing the full number. See our complete document checklist.

Petition and Schedules Questions

The trustee needs to confirm you understand and stand behind the documents filed with the court.

Questions About Your Filing

  1. Did you read your bankruptcy petition before it was filed?
  2. Did you sign the petition?
  3. Is everything in your schedules true and correct to the best of your knowledge?
  4. Do you need to make any corrections or amendments to your schedules?
  5. Did you list all of your assets - everything you own?
  6. Did you list all of your debts - everyone you owe money to?
  7. Did you list all of your income from all sources?

If you realize something is wrong or missing, tell the trustee. Honest corrections are far better than discovered omissions. Your attorney can file an amendment after the meeting.

Asset and Property Questions

The trustee is looking for non-exempt assets - property that could be sold to pay creditors. In Chapter 7, this is their primary function. In Chapter 13, they want to make sure your plan pays unsecured creditors at least as much as they would receive in a Chapter 7 liquidation.

Common Asset Questions

  1. Do you own any real estate? Any interest in any property?
  2. Do you own any vehicles? How many? What are the year, make, and model?
  3. Do you have any bank accounts? Checking, savings, money market?
  4. What were the balances in your bank accounts on the date you filed?
  5. Do you have any retirement accounts - 401(k), IRA, pension?
  6. Do you own any stocks, bonds, or investment accounts?
  7. Do you own any jewelry worth more than a few hundred dollars?
  8. Do you have any collections - coins, art, antiques, firearms?
  9. Are you owed money by anyone? Any pending lawsuits or insurance claims?
  10. Do you have an interest in any business?
  11. Have you inherited anything or do you expect to inherit anything?
  12. Do you own any tools of trade or professional equipment?

Income and Employment Questions

Income Questions

  1. Are you currently employed? Where do you work?
  2. How long have you worked there?
  3. What is your gross monthly income?
  4. Does anyone else contribute to household income?
  5. Do you receive any income from sources other than employment - Social Security, disability, pension, rental income, side jobs?
  6. Has your income changed since you filed?
  7. Are you expecting any increase in income in the near future - raise, new job, seasonal work?

Transfer and Payment Questions

These questions are designed to detect potential preferential transfers (payments to certain creditors before filing) and fraudulent transfers (moving assets to avoid creditors).

Transfer Questions

  1. Have you transferred any property to anyone in the last two years?
  2. Have you given away any property in the last two years?
  3. Have you sold any property for less than its fair market value in the last two years?
  4. Have you paid any creditor more than $600 in the 90 days before filing? (This is the preference threshold for non-insiders.)
  5. Have you paid any family member or business associate more than $600 in the last year? (Insider preference period is one year.)
  6. Have you repaid any loans from friends or family?
  7. Have you put any property in someone else's name?

Debt-Specific Questions

Questions About Your Debts

  1. What caused you to file bankruptcy?
  2. Do you owe any domestic support obligations - child support or alimony?
  3. Do you owe any taxes?
  4. Do you have any student loans?
  5. Are any of your debts the result of a lawsuit or judgment?
  6. Did you take on any new debt in the months before filing - credit cards, loans, cash advances?
  7. Did you make any large purchases in the 90 days before filing?

Chapter 13-Specific Questions

If you filed Chapter 13, the trustee will also ask about your repayment plan and budget.

Plan and Budget Questions (Chapter 13 Only)

  1. Can you afford your proposed monthly plan payment?
  2. Are your expenses accurate? Are there any changes since you filed?
  3. How do you plan to fund your plan payments?
  4. Are you current on your mortgage payments?
  5. Are you current on your vehicle payments?
  6. Have you filed all required tax returns?
  7. Are you current on any post-petition domestic support obligations?

How to Answer

The most important rules for answering trustee questions:

  • Be honest. You are under oath. Lying at a 341 meeting is perjury - a federal crime.
  • Be brief. Answer the question that was asked. Do not volunteer extra information.
  • Say "I don't know" if you don't know. Guessing is worse than admitting uncertainty.
  • Ask for clarification if you do not understand a question.
  • Let your attorney help. If you have counsel, they can clarify or rephrase a question.
  • Review your petition before the meeting so the answers are fresh in your mind.

Frequently Asked Questions

What questions does the trustee ask at a 341 meeting?

The trustee asks identity verification questions, questions about your bankruptcy petition and schedules, questions about your assets and property, income and employment questions, and questions about recent transfers or payments to creditors. The specific questions depend on your case, but the categories are the same in every district. Most meetings take 5 to 15 minutes.

How should I answer questions at the 341 meeting?

Answer honestly, briefly, and directly. You are under oath. Do not volunteer extra information - answer only the question asked. If you do not know the answer, say so. If you do not understand a question, ask the trustee to rephrase it. Do not guess.

Can the trustee ask about my spending habits?

Yes. The trustee may ask about recent large purchases, cash advances, balance transfers, or payments to specific creditors. They are looking for potential fraud, preferential transfers, or concealed assets. If you made any unusual transactions in the months before filing, be prepared to explain them honestly.
